### Design implications of "up to 20 players" — FLAG FOR PRD / ARCHITECTURE

- **Deck math:** 20 players × 1 card each pushes past one 52-card deck's comfortable range → the **1-vs-2-deck threshold** decision becomes a real requirement, not optional. (Already an open PRD question; now upgraded in importance.)
- **Showdown UX:** revealing/scanning ~20 cards to find the lowest is a materially different UI problem than 5 cards — the app likely needs to *compute and highlight* the loser(s) rather than rely on humans eyeballing it. Flag for PRD/UX.
- **Architecture load:** 20 concurrent WebSocket connections is still trivial for Cloudflare/Durable Objects — no concern, just noted (research scoped 3–10 but headroom is large).
- **Turn-loop pacing:** a full clockwise pass with 20 players is long; "~10 sec of screen attention per round" still holds per player, but total round wall-time grows — worth a PRD note on whether large tables stay fun.

- **Confidence:** High on the web versions being remote-oriented and on AirConsole/Jackbox models. Could not exhaustively crawl app stores (an obscure niche native app could exist); did not load live UIs to confirm the remote apps *forbid* in-person use (they could be repurposed at a table). Brief should not overclaim "no one has ever done this."
