# Story 2.4: Take a Turn — Swap or Keep (the two-button hero)

Status: review

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As an active Player (including the Reluctant Player),
I want exactly two big choices on my turn — Swap or Keep,
so that I can act instantly with zero confusion and shove my trash onto my neighbor (the squirm).

## Acceptance Criteria

> Source ACs verbatim from [epics.md#Story 2.4] (lines 569–603). The "**Then**" clauses are the binding contract; the AC IDs are this story's addressing scheme.

**AC-2.4.1 — Your Turn surface: neon active frame + two ≥72px pill primaries + peek control, nothing else (FR-6, UX-DR5, NFR-9)**
Given it becomes a Player's Turn,
When the engine routes them,
Then their device shows the **Your Turn surface** — the **neon active frame** (gentle ~1.2s pulse; **static under Reduce Motion**), **two massive (≥72px) pill primaries SWAP and KEEP** in the thumb zone, and a **peek control**; nothing else competes.

**AC-2.4.2 — Waiting surface: active Player's name in a static frame + own Lives, never a Card (FR-6, UX-DR6)**
Given an inactive Player,
When someone else is acting,
Then their device shows ONLY the **Waiting surface** — the active Player's name in a **static frame, no pulse, no motion**, plus **their own Lives**; never any Card value.

**AC-2.4.3 — SWAP exchanges with the Player to the right; turn passes right; value-free squirm signal (FR-6, AR-7)**
Given SWAP,
When the active Player taps it (**carrying the turn token**),
Then the active Player and the **Player to their right EXCHANGE Cards** (each then holds the other's former Card; everyone still holds exactly one), the **Turn passes right**, and the receiving Player's device shows a **value-free squirm signal** (`justReceivedSwap`) — which **carries no card data** and **re-passes the standing SM-6 test**.

**AC-2.4.4 — KEEP retains the Card; turn passes right; exactly ONE pass, no timer, no auto-advance (FR-6)**
Given KEEP,
When the active Player taps it,
Then their Card is **retained** and the **Turn passes right**; turn order is **exactly ONE pass** (Starting Player → Last Player, **no wrap**); there is **no timer and no auto-advance**.

**AC-2.4.5 — Reluctant-Player play-confirmed: complete a Turn unaided; card re-hides; debounce prevents double-fire (Pre-mortem C, NFR-8/SM-5)**
Given a genuine non-gamer at the table,
When they take a Turn unaided (peek → Swap/Keep),
Then they complete it without anyone leaning over to help, the **card re-hides cleanly**, and the **debounce prevents a double-fire** — **validated by play, not only unit test**.

**AC-2.4.6 — King is social-only: no King branch, no Swap-validation read of a non-owner's Card; standing test (FR-8, NFR-1, §11.1)**
Given the King is social-only (FR-8 — a negative requirement: absence of code, folded here per Amelia review),
When any Swap is validated,
Then the Swap is **NEVER refused on the basis of the target's Card value**, `validate.ts`/`engine.ts` contain **NO King-specific branch** and **NEVER read another Player's Card** to decide whether a Swap is allowed, and a **standing test asserts no Swap-validation path depends on a non-owner's hand** (the King is simply the highest value at Showdown). The table's "you can't dump on me" convention is honored socially; the app shows nothing.

**AC-2.4.7 — Accessibility floor on Your Turn: SWAP/KEEP are the first two focus stops; role+state labels; SR "Your turn" announce (NFR-10)**
Given the accessibility floor on Your Turn,
When the surface is built,
Then **SWAP and KEEP are the FIRST two focus stops** (focus order follows reading order), **every action is labeled with role + state**, and the **turn indicator announces "Your turn" to the screen reader on transition**.

## Dev Notes

### What this story IS / IS NOT
- **IS:** the **first FULL vertical slice of gameplay** — SERVER swap/keep (pure `applySwap`/`applyKeep` + turn advance via the EXISTING `nextAliveSeat`, the turn-scoped `checkTurnToken`/`bumpTurnToken` consumed for the FIRST time, the `not-your-turn` check, the `justReceivedSwap` value-free squirm producer) AND CLIENT (the real Your Turn two-button hero + the calm Waiting surface + the `sendSwap`/`sendKeep` seams + accessibility floor). Plus the **King social-only standing test** (FR-8 — a negative requirement realized as a test, folded here per Amelia review).
- **IS NOT:** the **peek reveal interaction** + `card-display.ts` rank→letter map (Story 2.5 — 2.4 ships only a peek-control PLACEHOLDER), the **Last-Player `drawFromDeck`** + the **`turns → allActed` transition** (Story 2.6 — 2.4 advances the turn for non-final seats; the Last Player's turn landing on `allActed` is 2.6's), `revealAll`/Showdown (Epic 3), the conductor-bar Deal button (Story 4.1), host-controls (Epic 4). The `drawFromDeck` intent STAYS in dispatch's default-reject set. No new `ErrorReason` is added (`not-your-turn` already exists, frozen). The ONLY possible `shared` edit is an OPTIONAL **memory-only** `Round` field for the squirm transient (server-only, NOT a wire change) — see Task 4.

### The accepted-path chokepoint — the TURN-scoped instance (copying 2.3's phase-scoped precedent)
`handleDeal` (2.3) set the precedent on the PHASE scope; `handleSwap`/`handleKeep` are the first TURN-scope consumers. The order, adapted:
```
shape-guard → table-null → round-null/phase-check → not-your-turn → checkTurnToken(round, token)
  → mutate (applySwap/applyKeep, advance currentTurnId) → bumpTurnToken(round) → [NO persist — round is memory-only] → [dispatch] fanOut
```
- **`checkTurnToken`/`bumpTurnToken` are the 2.2 primitives** (rules/validate.ts) — REUSE; keep them visibly adjacent (deferred-work #117 — honor the order by convention). The guard THROWS `stale-turn` on a mismatch (a double-tapped swap/keep) — caught by dispatch's single try/catch, turned into a targeted `error`, swallowed silently by the client (2.2). [validate.ts:43–45, 63–65; dispatch.ts:94–100.]
- **CRITICAL — null-round ordering (deferred-work #123):** the `round-null / phase==="turns"` gate MUST precede `checkTurnToken`, because `checkTurnToken(round, …)` reads `round.turnToken` and the D2.1 reload coercion leaves `round: null`. A turn intent after a coerced reload must reject as `phase-illegal` BEFORE the token deref. This is the explicit guidance 2.2's review left for THIS handler.
- **No persist (unlike deal):** a swap/keep changes ONLY memory-only `round` fields; the durable summary is unchanged (phase stays `turns`). So swap/keep are memory-only mutations — NO `persistSummary` call (consistent with `markDisconnected`'s no-persist precedent and AC-2.2.5). Document this so a reviewer does not flag a "missing persist." [architecture round-trip step 4 "persistSummaryIfPhaseChanged"; handlers.ts markDisconnected:413–419.]

### Engine helpers — pure, REUSE `nextAliveSeat` (do not reimplement)
- The swap TARGET and the NEXT turn-actor are the **same seat** ("Player to your right") — both are `nextAliveSeat(players, callerSeatIndex)`. So `applySwap` computes the neighbor ONCE and uses it for both the exchange and the turn advance. `nextAliveSeat` already exists (Story 2.3, engine.ts:74–88), sorts by `seatIndex`, walks right wrapping, skips non-`isAlive`. REUSE it. [architecture.md:316–318; 2.3 engine.ts.]
- **MVP turn-order skips on `isAlive` ONLY, never `isConnected`** — a disconnected-but-alive Player still owes a Turn; the Host conducts around them socially [architecture.md:325–328]. `nextAliveSeat` already enforces this; do NOT add an `isConnected` skip.
- **deferred-work #7** (`nextAliveSeat` returns a wrong-but-plausible seat on an unknown `fromSeatIndex`): 2.4 is a REUSE site flagged by 2.3's review. In 2.4 the caller's `seatIndex` is ALWAYS a real seat (the caller is `currentTurnId`, an alive seated player, verified by the not-your-turn check), so the unknown-index case is not reachable. Note it; you need not fix it here, but DO confirm the caller's seatIndex is looked up from the live roster (not trusted from the wire).
- **King (FR-8 / AC-2.4.6) — the ABSENCE of code is the requirement.** `applySwap` is an unconditional exchange. It MUST NOT read `Card.rank`/`Card.suit` of the neighbor (or anyone) to decide anything. There is NO `if (neighbor card is King) refuse` branch — the King's un-dumpability is a SOCIAL convention the app never enforces. This is also an SM-6 inference-channel obligation (deferred-work #52/#54): no value-dependent behavior.
