## What Went Well (lock in as deliberate practice)

- **"Verify the bets first, then the epic is trivial" — paid a direct dividend.** The Epic-4 retro *verified against live code* (not assumed) both binding prerequisites: resolution parameterized 2..20 (Decision #7, `engine.test.ts:598-639`) and `buildDeck(composition)` already input-driven (Decision #8, `engine.ts:47/131`). Result: Epic 5's entire functional gap was **one hardcoded constant → one pure mapping**, called at two deal sites. The epic was genuinely small, exactly as the prior retro predicted. The "verify first" discipline (Dennis's Epic-4 choice) is reaffirmed as deliberate practice.

- **Contract-up-front held for a FIFTH epic running.** Zero `types.ts` changes — even on the MVP-closer. `DeckComposition` stayed engine-internal as the architecture intended; no projection, no Intent, no `ErrorReason`, no router/client change. The Story-1.3 decision-#2 dividend is now five epics deep.

- **The review found what the build missed — again — and Dennis chose to fix it at the finish line.** The adversarial code review surfaced a *pre-existing* defect (NOT caused by 5.1): `handleDealAgain` had no `MIN_PLAYERS` floor, so a 1-player re-deal was reachable via Story-4.2 mid-session host-removal at `roundResult` (2 alive → remove 1 → 1 alive still `roundResult` → Re-deal → silent 1-player round). `assertDealable(1,{decks:1})` passes; the `roundResult` phase gate's stated ≥2-alive invariant is broken precisely by removal. **Dennis opted to fold the fix in at MVP close** rather than carry a known defect past the finish line. Added `if (aliveCount < MIN_PLAYERS) throw new IntentError("phase-illegal")`, completing the symmetry with `handleDeal`. **RED-first confirmed:** the new regression test (`table-server-host-controls.do.test.ts`, `dealAgain floor: …`) *timed out* without the guard (the 1-player re-deal proceeded silently, emitting NO error event) and passes with it. This is Epic-4's Action 2 ("state-mutating handlers get standing high-depth adversarial review") paying off on a handler the diff touched.

- **GATE-2 purity respected by placement, not by exception.** `compositionFor` was put in `server/src/rules/engine.ts` (the purity boundary) and keyed on the existing `SINGLE_DECK_MAX_PLAYERS = 10` constant — **no hardcoded literal `10`**, no reimplementation of deck building (reuses `buildDeck`). The mapping is data/config, not ad-hoc logic in `handlers.ts`.

- **The boundary test Decision #7 asked for was a real integration test.** A live deal at exactly 10 (1 deck / 42 leftover) and 11 (2 decks / 93 leftover) inspecting the *live* round — plus a 20-player smoke (20 carded, 84 leftover) — not a unit test of the mapping dressed up as integration.

Raised to 40. Worth remembering for any future >10-seat DO test.

## Key Lessons

1. **Paying down correctness early makes the dependent epic trivial.** Epic 3 parameterized resolution 2..20 *precisely so* Epic 5 would not reopen it; Epic 2 parameterized the deck *precisely so* the two-deck merge would be config, not surgery. Epic 5 collected that debt-paydown as a one-constant change. Front-loading the hard, reusable correctness work is the cheapest way to make the last mile easy.
2. **Fix known carried defects at the finish line, not "later."** Dennis chose to fold the pre-existing `handleDealAgain` floor bug into MVP close rather than ship a play-validated MVP with a known silent-1-player-round path. RED-first proof (timeout → pass) made it a confident, cheap fix.
3. **The adversarial review is the correctness layer even for "trivial" server-only diffs.** A one-constant change still warranted Blind + Edge + Acceptance layers — and they found a reachable, pre-existing edge the build/typecheck/tests all passed over. Review depth is not proportional to diff size; it is proportional to what the diff *touches* (here, a deal handler).
4. **A silent feature can still be tested at its true seam.** FR-13 ships no UI, but its correctness was pinned by a *live* deal at the 10↔11 boundary inspecting the real round — not a unit test of the pure mapping. Test the behavior at the integration seam, not just the function.
