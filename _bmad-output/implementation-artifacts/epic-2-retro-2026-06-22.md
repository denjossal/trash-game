## What Went Well (lock in as deliberate practice)

- **Contract-up-front paid a dividend (decision #2, from Epic 1).** Six stories, **zero wire-contract changes**. 2.6 closed the epic without touching `shared/src/types.ts` — `drawFromDeck`/`allActed`/`isLastPlayer` were all pre-named in 1.3; 2.6 only filled in producers.
- **The two-scope guard built WHOLE (2.2, decision #1) consumed cleanly downstream.** 2.3 = first phase-scope consumer; 2.4 = first turn-scope consumer; 2.6 = the hybrid that persists. Every handler copied the same `guard → mutate → bump → persist` shape `handleDeal` set as precedent. The consistency is *why* reviews came back clean.
- **SM-6 as a mechanical gate, not a vibe.** The negative-assertion test re-ran on every new projection field (`justReceivedSwap`, `isLastPlayer`); inference channels (b) timing-indistinguishability and (c) no-behavioral-tell were **closed** this epic with real value-independence regression tests (`applySwap`/`applyKeep`/`applyDraw` do constant work, no rank read; the Draw button is keyed only on the value-free `isLastPlayer`).
- **Tight-slice discipline, gates green every time, no scope creep.** 2.5 stayed client-only and resisted scaffolding Playwright; 2.4 shipped a deliberate peek *placeholder* and deferred cleanly to 2.5. Each story's notes name exactly what it IS and IS NOT.
- **Router-leak caught in build, not in prod.** 2.6's `allActed` transition would have routed the Starting Player to a phantom `yourTurn`; the dev caught it and cleared `currentTurnId` in the shared `maybeCompletePass` step, pinned by a DO test.

## What Was Hard (recurring patterns)

- **Manual-confirmed ACs are accumulating (the #1 theme).** Three play/manual ACs this epic with **no re-runnable harness**:
  - AC-2.3.4 — fast-start `<30s`: structural evidence only; real measure deferred to the 4.1 Deal button.
  - AC-2.4.5 — Reluctant-Player completes a turn unaided: *validated by play*, not testable in jsdom.
  - AC-2.5.2 — peek auto-hides on `visibilitychange`/`pagehide`: explicitly non-deterministic in jsdom (only `blur` is unit-tested); a six-point manual checklist lives in 2.5's notes.
  These are real properties of the product's soul (eyes-up, no-confusion, a phone set down never leaks a hand) and currently rest on a dev's recorded run. **Epic 3 adds two more** qualitative gates on top: the Showdown flip-safety (`≤400ms`, no strobe, Reduce-Motion) and pre-mortem A's joint squirm+reveal tuning in one live session.
- **Review→done tracking lag.** 2-4/2-5/2-6 shipped reviews but were never flipped to `done`, and `epic-2` was never closed — caught only by reconciling at the top of this retro. 2-1/2-2/2-3 each have a `done code-review` status line; the last three didn't.
- **Engine-primitive robustness gaps keep deferring forward.** `nextAliveSeat` on an unknown seat index, `dealRound` bounds, `dealAgain` trusting `startingPlayerId` — each correctly deferred (not reachable in scope), each tagged to Epic 3 (3.1/3.4). Owned, not lost — but **Epic 3 is where the bill comes due**: `dealAgain` (3.4) is the first caller that can pass a possibly-eliminated previous host.

## Key Lessons

1. **Qualitative ACs need a re-runnable home or they pile up.** "Validated by play / manual-confirmed" is honest, but three of them across one epic (plus two more coming in Epic 3) is a signal to invest in an e2e harness now, not later.
2. **A code review isn't "done" until the tracking says so.** The work-vs-bookkeeping gap nearly ran a retro against a half-open epic. The close step must flip the status, not just merge the commit.
3. **Contract-up-front + build-the-mechanism-whole keeps reviews clean.** Two Epic-1 bets (decisions #1, #2) are the structural reason Epic 2 had zero contract churn and copy-paste-consistent handlers.
4. **Forward-deferred primitives come due at their first real caller.** The engine gaps were safe to defer through Epic 2; Epic 3's `dealAgain` is the caller that finally exercises them — decide the assert-vs-trust question before 3.4.
