## What Went Well

- **The spike gate earned its keep (1-1, decision #9).** Gate-and-stop caught a real defect — `ctx.getWebSockets()` returns 0 for standard-mode (partyserver-default) sockets — *before* any product code. Without it, the GC would have deleted rooms full of live players. Correction shipped cleanly in Story 1.11.
- **Contract-up-front held (1-3, decision #2).** Full `TableState` + all seven `Phase` values defined once in `@trash/shared`; nothing downstream retrofitted `lives`/`isAlive`/`seatIndex`/`allActed`.
- **Privacy as a mechanical gate, not a vibe (1-4, decisions #3/E).** `projectStateFor` + the standing SM-6 negative-assertion test pin privacy to the engine seam and re-run in CI on every new projection field.
- **Honest deferral discipline.** Every shortcut logged with its risk and owning story (incl. the spike's own AC2 "false-PASS" verdict strings). Nothing hidden.
- **Test + CI gate real from the start (1-2).** ESLint privacy/purity bans go red-first; 73 tests green.

## What Was Hard (recurring patterns)

- **Socket lifecycle is the soft spot (most-repeated theme).** `maxRetries:0` + detached listeners → a dropped socket goes silent with no diagnostic. Flagged in **1-5, 1-7, 1-8, and 1-10** (`adoptSocket` leaks its message listener / double-subscribes on re-adopt). Owner: the reconnect/AR-12 flow.
- **Mutate-then-persist ordering is codebase-wide** (handlers.ts:113, 216, 292). Not atomic on a write failure; bounded by DO storage reliability. A convention, not a 1-x defect — revisit if `ctx.storage` becomes a real failure surface.
- **Name-content validation deferred (1-6, 1-7)** → correctly routed to the lobby-UI validation story.
- **Concurrency gaps the harness can't reach** — multi-device activation gate (pre-mortem B) and claim-on-create race rely on `wrangler dev` / a manual `.mjs`, not a re-runnable CI gate.

## Key Lessons

1. **A gate-and-stop spike pays for itself.** The single most valuable thing Epic 1 did was refuse to build until the riskiest Cloudflare assumptions were empirically checked. Keep this discipline for any future foundational premise.
2. **Define the whole contract before exercising any slice.** Naming `allActed`, `lives`, `winnerIds`, `newGame` up front means Epic 3 *extends* rather than *introduces* — fewer retrofits, cleaner privacy gate.
3. **Tag every deferral to its owner.** The `deferred-work.md` ledger is the reason none of these gaps are scary — each is a known, owned seam.
4. **Recurring single-theme debt deserves a single owner, not N patches.** The socket-liveness items should be fixed as one coherent reconnect-flow story, not piecemeal across surfaces.
